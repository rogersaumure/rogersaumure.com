About

I am a PhD candidate in Marketing at The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania. My research examines how technology — particularly large language models, social media, and translation tools — reshapes consumer expression, self-disclosure, and behavior change.

My research addresses three questions. First, when AI writes alongside us, what happens to our voice? Second, when consumers publish goal progress on social media, how does that affect goal pursuit? Third, can large language models be deployed to support healthy behavior change at scale?

Before Wharton, I earned a B.S. in Neuroscience (magna cum laude) at the University of Miami, with minors in Chemistry and Economics. My methodological toolkit includes unstructured data analysis, multilevel modeling, structural equation modeling, and field experiments.
