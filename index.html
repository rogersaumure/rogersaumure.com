About

I'm a PhD student in Marketing at The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania. My research sits at the intersection of consumer behavior and the technologies that increasingly mediate our lives — large language models, social platforms, and the translation layers that sit between us.

I ask three kinds of questions. First, when AI writes alongside us, what happens to our voice? Second, when we publish progress on social media — workouts logged, miles run, books finished — what does that do to the goal itself? Third, can we deploy LLMs as a force multiplier for healthy behavior change, at population scale?

Before Wharton I studied Neuroscience at the University of Miami, with minors in Chemistry and Economics. My methodological toolkit leans on unstructured data, multilevel and structural equation modeling, and field experiments.
